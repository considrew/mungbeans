🏆 Buffett Quality Screen

Companies meeting Warren Buffett's core investment criteria:

  • High ROE (> 15%) — efficient use of shareholder capital
  • Low Debt (D/E < 50%) — conservative balance sheet
  • Positive Free Cash Flow — real earnings, not accounting tricks
  • Profitable — actually making money

"A truly great business must have an enduring moat that protects excellent returns on invested capital." — Buffett

🎯 Yartseva Multibagger Screen

Based on statistical research of 464 stocks that 5-10x'd from 2009-2024:

  • Market cap < $2B (small caps dominated)
  • FCF Yield > 5% (cash generation matters most)
  • Book-to-Market > 0.40 (value pricing)
  • Positive equity

Combine with "Below Line" signal filter for the full Yartseva setup (near lows). Always verify improving margins and reinvestment quality.

👑 Dividend Aristocrats

S&P 500 companies with 25+ consecutive years of dividend increases:

  • Proven durability through multiple economic cycles
  • Management committed to returning capital
  • Usually have strong competitive moats

Aristocrats rarely go on sale. Combine with "Below Line" signal filter to find generational entry points.

🏰 Wide Moat Screen

Companies with durable competitive advantages:

  • Gross Margin > 40% — strong pricing power
  • ROE > 15% — efficient capital allocation
  • These margins indicate barriers to competition

"The key to investing is determining the competitive advantage of any given company and the durability of that advantage." — Buffett

🦈 Cannibals (Aggressive Buybacks)

Companies aggressively eating their own shares:

  • Share count reduced by > 5% over 3 years
  • Fewer shares = your slice of profits grows
  • Shows management confidence + shareholder alignment
  • Buffett calls these "cannibal" companies

"When stock can be bought below a business's value it is probably the best use of cash." — Buffett

⚠️ Diluters (Share Count Growing)

Companies increasing their share count:

  • Share count increased by > 2% over 3 years
  • Often from stock-based compensation or secondary offerings
  • Dilutes existing shareholders' ownership stake
  • Not always bad, but requires scrutiny

Watch for: excessive SBC, frequent capital raises, or acquisitions funded by stock.

🔍 Insider Buying

Corporate insiders making conviction purchases:

  • Open-market purchases over $500K in the last 12 months
  • Insiders have the deepest knowledge of a company's prospects
  • Buying with their own money signals confidence
  • Combine with "Below Line" signal filter for the strongest setup

"Insiders might sell their shares for any number of reasons, but they buy them for only one: they think the price will rise." — Peter Lynch

🔇 Quiet Accumulation

Stocks where volume is drying up and what remains skews toward up-weeks:

  • Relative Volume < 0.5x — sellers may be exhausted
  • Accumulation Ratio > 1.2 — buyers show more conviction on green weeks
  • Combine with "Below Line" signal filter to find discounted setups

Quiet accumulation often precedes a meaningful move. The combination of drying volume and buyer conviction is a setup worth watching.

📈 Growing Free Cash Flow

The business is generating more cash every year:

  • Free cash flow trending upward over recent years
  • Growing FCF can fund buybacks, dividends, or debt paydown
  • Combine with "Below Line" signal filter to find mispriced businesses

A stock below the line with growing cash flow is a business getting healthier while the market looks the other way.
